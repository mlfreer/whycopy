Welcome!

This study is conducted by a team of researchers from the University of Essex and founded by the Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC). The aim of this study is to explore decision making under risk. The study consists of a short survey and several decision tasks. The survey and the decision tasks should take about 30 minutes to complete. Please choose a quiet environment in order to remain focused and to avoid being interrupted while completing the survey. Also, please do not browse any other websites during the study. If you cannot guarantee that you will be focused, we ask you to please participate another time. Participating in this study will involve no cost to you. You will be paid £2.5 for taking part in our study. In addition, you will receive a payment depending on your answers and choices in the decision tasks.

Your participation in this study is purely voluntary, and you may withdraw your participation and your data at any time during the study and without any liability to you. You can withdraw from a study by returning your submission. (See here for more information) Your data will be kept completely confidential. The only data we use are your answers and decisions; we do not keep any other personal information. If you wish to participate, please select the Accept button below to begin the survey.

If you have any concerns about any aspect of the study or you have a complaint, in the first instance please contact the principal investigator of the project, Professor Simon Weidenholzer, (sweide@essex.ac.uk). If you are still concerned, you think your complaint has not been addressed to your satisfaction or you feel that you cannot approach the principal investigator, please contact the Director of Research in Economics at the University of Essex, Dr. Abhimanyu Gupta (a.gupta@essex.ac.uk), or the University of Essex’s Research Governance and Planning Manager, Sarah Manning-Press (sarahm@essex.ac.uk). Please include in your query the ERAMS reference number: ETH2122-0711.

Our Data Controller (dpo@essex.ac.uk) can give more information on compliance of the research with GDPR.
